About Me

I'm a learning designer who believes effective adult learning combines evidence-based instructional design with psychological safety and inclusive practice.

My journey started with educational psychology—studying how people learn, what motivates behavior change, and how to create environments where learning thrives. I taught English to diverse adult learners across cultural contexts, earning my DELTA qualification and discovering my passion for designing learning experiences, not just delivering them.

This led me to programme design and facilitation, where I combined instructional design frameworks with my psychology background. I've designed CPD-accredited training programmes achieving 95%+ satisfaction, led trauma-informed co-design with vulnerable communities, and developed learning resources for adult professionals.

What sets my work apart

  • Educational psychology expertise: Evidence-based understanding of how adults learn
  • Trauma-informed practice: Creating psychologically safe environments where all learners can thrive
  • Instructional design rigor: ADDIE, Bloom's Taxonomy, assessment design, evaluation frameworks
  • Facilitation mastery: Building trust, managing group dynamics, responsive adaptation
  • AI capabilities: Leveraging generative AI to enhance (not replace) human-centered learning

I'm particularly passionate about

  • Designing learning experiences for diverse adult populations
  • Creating inclusive, accessible learning that honors different learning needs
  • Working collaboratively with subject matter experts and faculty
  • Measuring impact beyond satisfaction scores—real behavior change and results
  • Making trauma-informed practice accessible to learning professionals

Currently, I'm contributing to UX for Change developing trauma-informed learning resources and exploring how AI can enhance learning design while maintaining human connection at the center.

I'm seeking opportunities to bring rigorous instructional design and trauma-informed facilitation to adult learning contexts—whether corporate training, higher education, professional development, or EdTech.
